PCI DSS Requirement 12: Support Information Security with Organizational Policies and Programs

Scope and Intent
Requirement 12 addresses the governance practices that make technical controls sustainable. It includes policies, risk management, roles and responsibilities, security awareness, incident response, service provider management, and targeted risk analyses used by other PCI DSS requirements.

Security Policy and Risk Program
Policies should describe management intent and operational responsibilities. Risk management should be active enough to keep policies, procedures, and control frequencies aligned with the environment rather than frozen in outdated assumptions.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.1.1 requires an overall information security policy to be established, published, maintained, and disseminated to relevant personnel and vendors. The policy must address the protection of account data.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.1.2 requires the information security policy to be reviewed at least once every 12 months and updated when the environment changes. The review keeps policy content aligned with current risks and operations.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.3.1 requires each targeted risk analysis to document the assets being protected, the threat being addressed, factors contributing to likelihood and impact, the resulting analysis, and how often the control will be performed. Targeted risk analyses are used where PCI DSS allows an entity-defined frequency.

Roles, Awareness, and Personnel Responsibilities
People must know the security responsibilities that apply to their roles. Awareness programs should reinforce secure handling of account data and the importance of reporting suspected incidents.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.4.1 requires executive management to establish responsibility for protecting account data and for a PCI DSS compliance program. Accountability must be assigned at a level able to sustain the program.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.5.2 requires PCI DSS scope to be documented and confirmed at least once every 12 months and after significant changes. Scope validation must identify all locations and flows of account data and all connected-to or security-impacting system components.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.6.1 requires a formal security awareness program to be implemented to make personnel aware of the entity's information security policy and procedures and their role in protecting account data.

Acceptable Use and Technology Controls
Technologies used by personnel can affect account data security. Policies and procedures should define acceptable uses, approval expectations, and security responsibilities for technologies that can store, process, transmit, or affect account data.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.2.1 requires acceptable use policies for end-user technologies to be defined and implemented. The policies must cover approval, acceptable uses, and a list of products approved by the company for employee use.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.3.3 requires cryptographic cipher suites and protocols in use to be documented and reviewed at least once every 12 months. The review must identify continued business need and security of the protocols.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.3.4 requires hardware and software technologies in use to be reviewed at least once every 12 months. The review must confirm continued support by the vendor and continued business need.

Incident Response
An incident response plan should be documented, tested, and understood before an incident occurs. The plan should define roles, communications, containment, evidence handling, and legal or payment brand notification steps where applicable.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.10.1 requires an incident response plan to be documented and implemented. The plan must be ready to respond immediately to a suspected or confirmed security incident.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.10.2 requires the incident response plan to be reviewed, tested, and updated at least once every 12 months. Testing helps confirm that personnel can execute the plan.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.10.5 requires the incident response plan to include monitoring and response to alerts from security monitoring systems. Alerts from tools such as intrusion detection, change detection, and anti-malware must feed response processes.

Service Provider Management
Organizations that use service providers remain responsible for understanding how account data is protected. Written agreements, due diligence, monitoring, and responsibility matrices help clarify which party performs each control.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.8.1 requires a list of all third-party service providers with which account data is shared or that could affect the security of account data to be maintained. The list supports governance of outsourced services.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.8.2 requires written agreements with service providers that include an acknowledgement of the provider's responsibility for the security of account data or the security-impacting services they provide. The acknowledgement clarifies accountability.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 12.8.5 requires information to be maintained about which PCI DSS requirements are managed by each service provider and which are managed by the entity. This responsibility matrix helps prevent control gaps.

Operational Evidence
Evidence for Requirement 12 includes approved policies, annual review records, targeted risk analyses, scope validation worksheets, security awareness training records, acceptable use acknowledgements, technology review records, incident response exercises, service provider lists, written agreements, and responsibility matrices.
