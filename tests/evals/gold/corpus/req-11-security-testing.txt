PCI DSS Requirement 11: Test Security of Systems and Networks Regularly

Scope and Intent
Requirement 11 verifies that security controls continue to work and that vulnerabilities are found before attackers exploit them. Testing includes wireless detection, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, intrusion detection, change detection, and validation of segmentation.

Wireless Detection
Wireless technologies can create unintended paths into the cardholder data environment. Entities need a way to identify authorized and unauthorized wireless access points and respond when unexpected devices are found.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.2.1 requires authorized and unauthorized wireless access points to be managed. The entity must maintain an inventory of authorized wireless access points and have a process to detect and identify unauthorized wireless access points.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.2.2 requires an inventory of authorized wireless access points, including a documented business justification. The inventory supports investigation of unknown or rogue wireless devices.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.2.3 requires detection of unauthorized wireless access points at least once every three months. If automated monitoring is used, it must generate alerts that personnel respond to.

Vulnerability Scanning
Internal and external vulnerability scans help identify weaknesses in systems that could affect the cardholder data environment. Scans should be repeated until high-risk findings are resolved, and external scans must be performed by an Approved Scanning Vendor when required.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.3.1 requires internal vulnerability scans to be performed at least once every three months and after significant changes. High-risk and critical vulnerabilities must be resolved and rescanned to verify remediation.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.3.1.2 requires authenticated internal vulnerability scans to be performed. Authenticated scanning improves visibility into missing patches and insecure configurations.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.3.2 requires external vulnerability scans to be performed at least once every three months by a PCI SSC Approved Scanning Vendor. Passing scan results must be achieved for each scan cycle.

Penetration Testing and Segmentation
Penetration testing evaluates whether vulnerabilities and control weaknesses can be exploited. Segmentation testing confirms that controls intended to isolate the cardholder data environment actually block access from out-of-scope networks.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.4.1 requires a penetration testing methodology that is defined, documented, and implemented. The methodology must cover industry-accepted approaches and the entire cardholder data environment perimeter and critical systems.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.4.2 requires internal penetration testing at least once every 12 months and after significant changes. The test must be performed by a qualified internal resource or qualified external third party.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.4.3 requires external penetration testing at least once every 12 months and after significant changes. The test must include the exposed external perimeter and critical systems.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.4.5 requires segmentation controls to be tested at least once every 12 months and after significant changes when segmentation is used to reduce PCI DSS scope. The testing must verify that segmentation is operational and effective.

Intrusion Detection and Change Detection
Testing is not limited to scheduled scans. Entities also need monitoring technologies that detect suspicious network traffic and unauthorized changes to critical files. Alerts should lead to investigation and response.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.5.1 requires intrusion-detection or intrusion-prevention techniques to detect or prevent intrusions into the network. Alerts generated by these techniques must be monitored and responded to by personnel.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.5.2 requires a change-detection mechanism to alert personnel to unauthorized modification of critical files. The mechanism must perform critical file comparisons at least once every seven days.

PCI DSS v4.0 Requirement 11.6.1 requires a change- and tamper-detection mechanism for payment pages. The mechanism must alert personnel to unauthorized changes to HTTP headers and payment page content as received by the consumer browser.

Operational Evidence
Evidence for Requirement 11 includes wireless inventories, rogue access point detection results, internal scan reports, ASV scan attestations, remediation tickets, penetration test reports, segmentation test methods and results, IDS or IPS alerts, file integrity monitoring configurations, and payment page change-detection alerts.
